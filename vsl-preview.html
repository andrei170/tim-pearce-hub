Section 01 - The 30-Second Open

On screen: Tim at the desk. Clean frame. Subtle drtimpearce.com brand colour palette in the background.

If you are watching this on the homepage, you are probably here because you saw a reel or a video and you are deciding whether one of these courses is the right next step. The next 5 minutes is the structured answer.

I am going to walk you through who each course is for, where in your injector journey each one slots in, and what the 90-second scorecard at the bottom of this page does to route you to the right tier without you having to guess.

Quick note before we start. Everything in this video is education for medical professionals. It is not patient-facing advice and it is not a guarantee about what any individual course will do for any individual practitioner. The standard regulatory frame applies and you will see it in the footer of every page on this site.

Section 02 - The Operator-Educator Footing (45 Seconds)

On screen: Tim at the desk. Cut-in: aggregate authority strip (23,927+ injectors trained worldwide / 1000+ five-star reviews / #1 aesthetics YouTube show / 15 years injecting experience / 25,000+ injectable treatments performed).

I have been injecting for 15 years and I have done over 25,000 treatments. We have trained over 23,927 injectors worldwide through this platform. The YouTube show is the longest-running aesthetics teaching channel I know of. None of this is the reason to take a course here. It is the reason to trust that the curriculum is built by someone who has spent two decades on the chair and inside the complications protocol.

If you have just qualified to inject, if you are building case load, or if you are running a clinic and looking for the operator-development layer above the curriculum, one of the next 5 minutes is for you.

Section 03 - The Three Practitioner Bands (90 Seconds)

On screen: Tim at the desk. Cut-in: simple text overlay naming each band as Tim describes it.

Three bands. Pick the one that sounds like you.

Band one. The new-to-injectables medical professional. You are qualified or close to qualified. You have not built case load yet. You are anxious about the first 100 procedures, the consultation flow, and the complications protocol you do not yet have. The Foundation Course is built for you. It is the structured entry into the discipline. Toxin first. Curriculum and safety scaffolding for the first wave of patients.

Band two. The intermediate injector building case load. You are injecting. You have built some case load. You are seeing the occasional bruise, the occasional asymmetry, the occasional vascular concern. You do not have a structured complications protocol. You do not have a peer room to surface a near-miss with. The Complications Course and the Filler Complications Course are the safety floor under your repertoire. They are the books you wish you had had when you started.

Band three. The senior injector building a clinic. You are injecting at volume. You are running a clinic or building one. The next bottleneck is not technique. It is case-load mix, retention, hiring, supervising other injectors, the brand build, and the operator-development layer above the curriculum. The Graduate Cohort is the application-gated step above the asynchronous catalogue. The cohort is not on the shop. It sits on its own application surface and you reach it via the scorecard.

Section 04 - The Scorecard As The Router (45 Seconds)

On screen: Tim at the desk. Cut-in: a quick on-screen preview of the scorecard interface (3-4 sample questions cycling).

At the bottom of this page you will find a 90-second scorecard. 10 to 12 questions. Years injecting, products injected today, complications training to date, clinic surface, top bottleneck right now, goal in the next 90 days.

The scorecard returns a readiness band and a one-click route to the matched course tier. If the scorecard returns Cohort-Ready, it routes you to the Graduate Cohort application. If it returns Too Early, it routes you to the Free Full Face Filler Web Class and the Foundation Course (the right first step, instead of a four-figure complications course that would be wasted on someone three months from injecting their first patient).

The point of the scorecard is to keep you from buying the wrong tier. It is the cheapest 90 seconds you will spend on this site.

Section 05 - The Course Walk (90 Seconds)

On screen: Tim at the desk. Cut-in: a still card per course as Tim names it.

Quick walk through the catalogue.

BOTOX Foundation Course. The structured entry. Toxin curriculum, first-procedure scaffolding, consultation flow, the early complications protocol introduction. Best-selling course on the platform.

Botulinum Toxin Complications. The safety floor for the toxin-injecting practitioner. Recognition, escalation, response. 6-month payment plan available.

Dermal Filler Complications. The safety floor for the filler-injecting practitioner. Vascular complications, escalation protocols, the case library. 6-month payment plan available.

8D Lip Design and Elective Lip Reversal. Technique series for the practitioner already injecting lips and looking for refinement plus the reversal protocol.

Longevity. The horizon course for the practitioner moving from procedural-only into the longevity-medicine conversation with patients.

Every course is asynchronous. You drill at your own pace. The complications curriculum is the spine; everything else is technique on top.

Section 06 - What Is NOT On The Shop (30 Seconds)

On screen: Tim at the desk.

What is not on the shop. The Graduate Cohort. That tier is application-gated, capped at 8 to 12 practitioners per cycle, and routed through the scorecard. The cohort is the operator-development layer above the asynchronous courses. If you are at that band, the scorecard will tell you. The application is 4 minutes. Manual review within 48 hours.

Section 07 - The Regulatory Frame (30 Seconds)

On screen: Tim at the desk. Cut-in: the educational footer copy on screen.

One last frame before the CTA. Every course on this platform is education for medical professionals. Aesthetic medical procedures carry inherent risks. Outcomes vary by patient, by practitioner, and by the consultation and consent process behind every treatment. Nothing on this site is regulated medical advice and nothing on this site is a patient-side guarantee. Consult a qualified medical professional in your jurisdiction.

If you are a practitioner watching this, you already know the frame. I am stating it because it sits behind every piece of teaching that happens on this platform.

On screen: Tim at the desk. Cut-in: scorecard CTA button.

Take the 90-second scorecard. It returns the right course tier for where you inject today. If you are the senior injector at the Cohort-Ready band, the scorecard routes you to the application. The button is right below this video. See you on the other side.
