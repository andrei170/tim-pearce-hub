Bottleneck Analysis - Dr Tim Pearce

Prepared: 2026-05-17 Lead diagnostic doc. This is the wedge. Four structural gaps between an order-of-magnitude practitioner audience and a flat asynchronous course catalogue. Each gap is named, sized, ranked, and mapped to the AG1 service that patches it.

The One-Sentence Read

~382,000 Instagram followers plus the #1 aesthetics YouTube show plus 23,927+ injectors already trained worldwide route to a flat eLearning shop with no VSL pre-sell, no readiness scorecard between the audience and the catalogue, no application-gated graduate cohort above the asynchronous courses, and no segmented welcome flow underneath the existing buyer base. Four patches install on top of the existing brand without changing the courses, the shop, the voice, or the regulatory footing.

Bottleneck 01 - The Pre-Sell Bridge Is Missing (HIGHEST LEVERAGE)

The Gap

The buy decision currently happens on a flat course-card grid. No long-form video pre-sell above the homepage hero. No long-form video pre-sell above any per-course buy page. The operator-educator voice that built the channel (the 15-year injector credential, the 25,000+ treatments register, the structured technique teaching) does not appear on the surface where the buy actually happens.

Why It Matters

The Instagram channel at ~382K followers and the YouTube show at 8 million cumulative views are the single highest-leverage assets in the entire stack. Every cold visitor arriving on drtimpearce.com has already opted into Tim's voice. The shop strips that voice out and replaces it with a card grid. The buyer who watched a technique reel in Tim's structured, calm, technique-led register lands on a catalogue that could belong to any eLearning Shopify-style storefront. The register break is the leak.

The Math (Conservative Floor)

Pre-sell VSL lift on existing eLearning checkout flows runs 8-18% incremental on cold visitors who currently bounce off a static catalogue. On an audience of this scale plus the existing 23,927+ trained-injector base who would re-buy adjacent courses, even a low-single-digit recovery on the catalogue surface compounds to a meaningful incremental revenue line on the existing course set alone, before any new tier exists.

The Fix - AG1 Service Map

We build the 5-7 minute VSL in Tim's operator-educator register. Script in Tim's voice (calibrated off 30 minutes of channel transcript), direct the record, edit the cut, embed on drtimpearce.com hero AND above the BOTOX Foundation Course buy page AND above the Botulinum Toxin Complications buy page AND above the Dermal Filler Complications buy page AND above the Lip Design surface. The voice on screen is Tim's. Captions on. Same VSL drives the cohort tier landing later.

The VSL respects the regulatory tone: no income claim, no patient-side promise, no advertising of prescription-only medicine to a consumer audience, no before-and-after outcome guarantee. The register is "for the practitioner, by the practitioner."

Bottleneck 02 - No Qualification Step Between Three Practitioner Bands And One Catalogue

The Gap

Three different practitioner bands land on the same flat catalogue: the new-to-injectables medical professional, the intermediate injector building case load, and the senior injector building a clinic. The shop does not ask "where are you in your injector journey, what do you inject today, what is the next 90 days, what is the bottleneck right now," and it does not route to a tier matched to that answer. Every viewer self-selects from the same grid with the same friction.

Why It Matters

Routing by readiness band is the single cheapest conversion lift on a multi-band practitioner audience. The new injector needs the Foundation Course plus the Complications curriculum. The intermediate injector needs the technique series plus the Filler Complications safety scaffolding. The senior injector building a clinic needs the graduate cohort, the operator-development layer, and the clinic-builder cadence - none of which is visible on the cold-touch path today. The buyer that wanted the wrong thing buys nothing, refunds, or drops out of the catalogue.

The Output

A 90-second "Are You Injection-Ready For This Course Tier?" scorecard. 10-12 questions across: years injecting (none / 0-1 / 1-3 / 3-5 / 5+), products injected today (toxin only / toxin + filler / toxin + filler + advanced / full repertoire), prescriber status, complications training to date, clinic surface (employed / chair-rent / clinic-owner / multi-site), top bottleneck right now (foundation gap / complications gap / technique refinement / clinic-builder gap), and goal in the next 90 days. Output: a readiness band readout (Too Early For This Tier / Ready For The Foundation Course / Ready For Complications / Ready For The Graduate Cohort + Application) PLUS a one-click routed recommendation to the matched buy surface (or to the cohort application if the readiness score lands in the top band).

The Fix - AG1 Service Map

We install the scorecard end-to-end: question logic, scoring algorithm, routing rules, readout copy in Tim's voice, email capture, readiness-band tagging on the ESP, embed across the Instagram bio link, the YouTube channel description, the homepage hero, and the per-course page sidebar. The readout is in Tim's voice. The routing is one click to the matched buy surface.

The scorecard does four things simultaneously:

  • Filters fit-mismatched buyers out of the wrong-tier course before they purchase, which lifts review quality and reduces refund / chargeback risk on the Complications and Filler Complications tiers
  • Captures email at scale from the ~382K IG and the YouTube audience for the first time, tagged at the moment of capture by readiness band
  • Tags the warm slice (senior injector, clinic-owner band, complications-trained) for the graduate cohort tier
  • Routes the early-stage slice back to the Free Full Face Filler Web Class plus the Foundation Course (the right first step) instead of into a four-figure course they will refund-request

Bottleneck 03 - No Application-Gated Graduate Cohort Tier Above The Asynchronous Courses

The Gap

The ladder tops out at the asynchronous Dermal Filler Complications course. The ~382K Instagram audience plus the YouTube audience plus the 23,927+ trained-injector base plus the existing Profinity Academy reference plus the WhatsApp practitioner cohorts cited in the testimonial wall are the warmest possible list for a tier above the asynchronous catalogue - and the publicly routed funnel between that audience and that tier does not exist. Adjacent aesthetic-medicine educators with an order of magnitude less reach run application-gated graduate cohorts at the senior end of their ladders.

Why It Matters

The graduate cohort tier is the highest-margin revenue line in any practitioner-education category. The asynchronous courses convert at low-to-four-figure GBP per seat. The graduate cohort converts the top 1-2% of the audience at a mid-tier price point with a capped cohort cap that creates structural scarcity. The same hour of operator time delivers an order of magnitude more revenue per seat. The Profinity Academy reference suggests the cohort already informally exists - the work is to productise it, gate it with an application, route to it from the scorecard, and put a public landing page underneath it.

Working Names And Format Options

Two productisable directions, pick on kickoff:

  1. The Aesthetic Practitioner Graduate Cohort. For the intermediate-to-senior injector who has completed the Complications and Filler Complications curriculum or holds equivalent training and wants the live, capped, application-gated step. 8-12 verified practitioners per cycle. Application-gated via Typeform. 8-week cohort with weekly live group calls in Tim's register, the existing Complications and Filler Complications libraries as the in-cohort safety drill kit, a private practitioner channel, two office-hours blocks per month, and a "graduate clinical scaffolding" sign-off at the end.
  2. The Aesthetic Clinic-Builder Cohort. For the senior injector building or scaling a clinic surface. 8-12 verified clinic-owners per cycle. Application-gated via Typeform. 8-week cohort built around the Business resource cluster plus the SkinViva clinic-builder track-record, with case-load mix, retention systems, brand build, hiring and supervision, and the clinic-systems scaffolding that sits behind a multi-million pound run-rate.

Both can run in parallel. Pick one for the inaugural cycle, layer the second in cycle two if cycle one fills.

The Math (Year-1 Floor Math, Conservative)

4 cycles per year, 10 verified entrants per cycle, mid-tier graduate-cohort pricing in the four-figure-per-seat band. Year-1 floor sits in the mid-six-figure range on a single cohort track, with operator delivery time of one weekly call plus two office-hours blocks per month. The second cohort track (if layered) doubles that line. The math is conservative because the cohort cap can lift to 12-16 once the cycle rhythm is in.

The Fix - AG1 Service Map

We build the graduate cohort tier end-to-end: cohort landing page + the Typeform application + the application scoring / approval workflow + the post-application email sequence + the cohort onboarding sequence + the alumnus surface for cycle-two recruitment + the Profinity Academy bridge for graduate retention. Tim runs the cohort calls. The cohort is the operator step above the asynchronous catalogue.

Bottleneck 04 - No Segmented Welcome Flow Underneath The Existing Buyer Base

The Gap

The Foundation Course buyer, the Complications buyer, the Filler Complications buyer, the Lip Design buyer, and the Free Web Class opt-in all route into the same generic post-purchase email. There is no observable buyer-tier segmentation on the eLearning purchase event surfaced to a cold visitor. The Filler Complications buyer who paid four figures gets the same post-purchase nurture as the Foundation Course buyer who paid low three figures. The Free Web Class opt-in who has not yet bought gets the same drip as a buyer who has.

Why It Matters

The 23,927+ trained-injector base is the warmest possible audience for the graduate cohort tier above it. Treating the Filler Complications buyer the same as the Foundation Course buyer caps the upgrade path before it exists. The Foundation buyer has a different next step (the Complications course). The Complications buyer has a different next step (the Filler Complications course or the technique series). The Filler Complications buyer has a different next step (the graduate cohort application). None of those next steps fire today because the welcome flow does not segment.

The Fix - AG1 Service Map

We wire the eLearning purchase webhook handler that tags every buyer by product (Free Web Class / Foundation / Complications / Filler Complications / Lip Design / Longevity) and forks the welcome sequence per tier. Per-tier sequence:

  • Free Web Class opt-in: Day 0 web class delivery. Day 3 first technique-led nurture. Day 7 Foundation Course invitation. Day 14 scorecard route. Day 30 inactive-warm re-touch.
  • Foundation Course buyer: Day 0 welcome plus first-procedure scaffolding. Day 7 complications-curriculum cross-sell. Day 14 technique-series invitation. Day 30 readiness-band check-in.
  • Complications buyer: Day 0 welcome plus safety drill cadence. Day 7 Filler Complications invitation. Day 14 graduate-cohort waitlist invitation. Day 30 office-hours invite.
  • Filler Complications buyer: Day 0 welcome plus advanced safety scaffolding. Day 7 graduate-cohort application invitation. Day 14 cohort-waitlist re-touch. Day 30 Profinity bridge.
  • Lip Design / Longevity buyer: Day 0 welcome. Day 7 technique-stack invitation. Day 14 graduate-cohort gateway.

Each sequence in Tim's register. Each call-to-action one click. The Filler-Complications-to-cohort upgrade is the line that does not exist today.
