5. Audience Composition (Triangulated from Catalogue + Promotion Surface)

Three buyer bands inside the same audience:

  1. The new-to-injectables medical professional. Drawn by the BOTOX Foundation Course. Doctor, dentist, pharmacist prescriber, or aesthetic nurse looking for the structured entry into the discipline. Wants the foundation curriculum, a safe first procedure, and the confidence to charge.
  2. The intermediate injector building case load. Drawn by 8D Lip Design, Elective Lip Reversal, the Complications courses, and the technique series. Already injecting. Looking for refinement, safety scaffolding, and a wider procedural repertoire.
  3. The senior injector building a clinic or a brand. Drawn by the Business resource cluster, the Longevity course, and the Profinity Academy reference. Already running a clinic surface, looking for the operator-development layer (case load mix, retention, clinic systems, brand) rather than another technique course.

The current catalogue serves the first two bands with productised asynchronous courses. The third band is served informally (Profinity Academy reference, WhatsApp cohorts in testimonial copy) but has no publicly productised tier with an application surface, a price band, and a graduation cadence.

8. Competitive Surface (Adjacent Aesthetic eLearning)

A non-exhaustive scan of adjacent UK and US aesthetic eLearning surfaces verifies that the application-gated graduate cohort tier and the productised clinic-builder cohort are common at the senior end of the category. Several adjacent educators publish a cohort intake (Typeform / Calendly / waitlist) above their asynchronous course catalogue and route the cohort buyers through a separate landing page with a visible cap and a visible cycle cadence. drtimpearce.com does not currently publish this surface despite carrying an order of magnitude more Instagram reach (~382K followers) than several of the adjacent operators who do.

The wedge is not "there is a new market" - the wedge is "the audience to fill a cohort tier already exists at this scale, the cohort already informally exists (Profinity Academy reference, WhatsApp practitioner cohorts in testimonial copy), and the publicly routed funnel between the audience and a cohort sign-up does not exist."

9. Regulatory Surface (Mandatory Context)

Aesthetic medicine eLearning sits inside an actively regulated environment in both the UK and the US.

  • UK: General Medical Council guidance on cosmetic interventions, JCCP register for non-surgical cosmetic practitioners, Care Quality Commission registration where applicable, ASA / CAP guidance on prescription-only-medicine advertising including botulinum toxin.
  • US: FDA scope on injectable products plus state-level medical board scope on who can inject and supervise.
  • EU: equivalent scope across national regulators.

Every public surface in this package stays at the level of "education for medical professionals" and avoids:

  • Income claims for the injector ("make £X with this course").
  • Direct procedural advice to the consumer ("you should get filler in your lips").
  • Before-and-after outcome promises framed as guarantees.
  • Anything that could be construed as advertising prescription-only medicine to a consumer audience.

Footer disclaimer locked on every public AG1-built surface: "Educational content only. Not regulated medical advice. Aesthetic medical procedures carry inherent risks; outcomes vary. Consult a qualified medical professional in your jurisdiction."
