Foundation

Copy Strategy - Dr Tim Pearce

Prepared: 2026-05-17 Foundation doc. Voice rules, niche-vocabulary bans, register separation, and locked hero claims across the four-piece install.

1. Voice Calibration

Source for voice calibration: 30 minutes of channel transcript, sampled across the technique-led explainer franchise (the procedure walk-throughs), the complications franchise (the safety scaffolding teaching), and the Instagram reel surface (the short-form practitioner-to-practitioner cadence).

Voice attribute Rule
Register Operator-educator. Pedagogy-first. Calm, structured, deliberately patient. Technique-led.
Diction UK English spelling and idiom (favour over favor, organisation over organization, whilst over while where natural).
Pace Short to medium sentences. One concept per sentence. No nested clauses.
Personality Topic-led, not personality-led. The hook is the procedure or the complication being taught, not the operator.
Authority Clinical credential footing (MB ChB BSc MRCGP, 15 years injecting, 25,000+ treatments). Foregrounded once per surface, never name-dropped to the point of self-promotion.
Humour Dry, occasional. Never at the expense of the topic. Never lifestyle-coded.

The voice on every AG1-built surface matches the channel. Buyer who arrives from a reel should not feel a register break on the homepage, the scorecard, the cohort landing, or the welcome flow.

2. Banned Vocabulary (Niche-Specific)

These words break the operator-educator register and trigger regulatory exposure on a regulated medical-credential surface. None appear in any AG1-built copy.

  • "Make six figures with aesthetics." "Earn £X per month." "Build a wealthy clinic." Any income claim, full stop. (UK ASA / CAP guidance plus US FTC scope.)
  • "Anti-ageing." "Reverse the years." "Look younger." Any patient-side outcome promise framed as a guarantee.
  • "Before and after." Standalone framing without informed-consent and risk disclosure. (Allowed only when paired with safety scaffolding language in technique education.)
  • "Pain-free." "Risk-free." "Zero complications." Anything that misrepresents the procedural risk profile.
  • "Best in the world." "The only injector who." Superlative claims that breach GMC Good Medical Practice on personal promotion.
  • "Botox party." Any consumer-event framing. Brand register is practitioner-education, not consumer-event.
  • "Cheap." "Discount filler." "Bargain toxin." Anything that frames prescription-only medicine as a consumer commodity.
  • "Get-rich-quick from aesthetics." Anything that frames the discipline as a shortcut.
  • "Game-changer." "Revolutionary." "Unlock." "Level up." "Leverage." Standard AI-slop bans.

3. Register Separation

Three surfaces, three registers. Adjacent, not interchangeable.

Surface Register Why
Instagram + YouTube + drtimpearce.com hero Tim's editorial. Operator-educator, pedagogy-first, technique-led, clinical-credential footing. The audience-facing surface. The voice that built the channel.
AG1-built funnel surfaces (VSL, scorecard, cohort landing, welcome flow, hub, landing page) Voice-matched to Tim's editorial. Same diction, same pace, same clinical patience. The buy-decision surfaces. Must feel like a continuation of the channel.
AG1-built outreach to Tim (DM, LinkedIn, email) Operator-to-operator. Direct, observational, peer-to-peer. Not channel-voice. These are AG1's voice talking to a senior MD operator. Different audience, different register.

The outreach surfaces do NOT try to mimic Tim's voice. They speak operator-to-operator about the FUNNEL, not about the channel.

5. Outcome Language (Non-Regulated, Non-Promise)

Outcomes are framed at the practitioner-development and operator-systems level. Never at the patient-outcome level, never at the income-outcome level.

Outcome we describe Outcome we do NOT describe
"Concept clarity on the toxin and filler curriculum." "Your patients will love their results."
"Safety scaffolding the cohort can drill weekly." "Zero-complications clinic guaranteed."
"A complications-response protocol the room reviews together." "Your patients will never experience complications."
"Plan adherence on the consultation cadence." "You will earn £X from this course."
"Clinic-builder systems for case-load mix and retention." "Build a six-figure clinic in 90 days."
"Graduation criteria on the safety-checklist and consultation-record adherence over a 60-day window." "You will be the best injector in your city."
"A peer room of 8-12 practitioners working at the same band." "Become an aesthetic millionaire."

6. Three Persona-Matched Hooks

These hooks appear in the scorecard readout and in the segmented welcome flow opener emails. Lock them on day one.

Persona 01 - The New-To-Injectables Medical Professional

Hook line: "The Foundation Course is the structured entry into the discipline." Pain pattern: Just qualified to inject (or about to qualify), no live case load yet, no complications protocol, anxious about the first 100 procedures. Promised outcome: Foundation curriculum + first-procedure scaffolding + complications-protocol introduction + safe entry into chair work.

Persona 02 - The Intermediate Injector Building Case Load

Hook line: "The Complications and Filler Complications curriculum is the safety floor under your repertoire." Pain pattern: Already injecting, building case load, occasional bruise / asymmetry / vascular concern, no structured complications protocol, no peer room to surface near-misses. Promised outcome: Complications curriculum + filler-specific safety scaffolding + protocol library + the technique-refinement series.

Persona 03 - The Senior Injector Building A Clinic

Hook line: "The Graduate Cohort is the operator-development step above the asynchronous curriculum." Pain pattern: Injecting at volume, running or building a clinic surface, the next bottleneck is no longer technique - it is case-load mix, retention systems, clinic build, hiring and supervising other injectors, brand build, and the operator-development layer. Promised outcome: Application-gated peer room + clinic-builder scaffolding + operator-development cadence + the Profinity bridge for graduate retention.

10. Quality Gates

Every AG1-built surface for Tim runs through this gate before publish:

  • No em-dashes anywhere except the literal sign-off pattern "- Andrei".
  • No standard AI-slop banned phrases (game-changer, revolutionary, unlock, leverage synergies, seamlessly, effortlessly, elevate your, etc.).
  • No income claims for the injector.
  • No patient-side outcome promises framed as guarantees.
  • No advertising of prescription-only medicine to a consumer audience.
  • Educational footer present on every public surface.
  • No GBP currency symbol on the landing page (UK currency cleanliness rule).
  • No USD currency symbol on the landing page (cold first-touch hint discipline).
  • UK English diction across every surface.

11. Educational Footer (Mandatory)

Educational content only. Not regulated medical advice. Aesthetic medical procedures carry inherent risks; outcomes vary. Consult a qualified medical professional in your jurisdiction.
